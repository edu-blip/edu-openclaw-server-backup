# LinkedIn Outreach Sequence — Rhetoric

**Target:** Series A+ B2B tech founders & C-level execs (raised >$10M, not healthcare)
**Tool:** Botdog (automated sequence)

---

## Pre-Connection Warm-Up (Botdog automation)

Before sending the connection request:
1. **Day 1:** View their profile
2. **Day 2:** Like or comment on a recent post (genuine, not generic)
3. **Day 3:** Send connection request

This makes you a familiar face before the ask. Acceptance rates jump 2-3x.

---

## Connection Request Message

> I'd love to manage your LinkedIn content.

*(46 chars — shows fully in preview. Short = curiosity. The ones who want this will reply.)*

**Keep this as-is.** It's working and the brevity is the feature.

---

## Follow-Up 1 — Day 3 after acceptance

> Hey {{first_name}}, thanks for connecting! Quick question — are you currently posting on LinkedIn consistently, or is it one of those "I know I should but..." situations?

*(Sets up the pain point without pitching. Gets them talking.)*

---

## Follow-Up 2 — Day 7 after acceptance

> No worries if you're slammed. Just wanted to share what we do in case it's relevant: we ghostwrite LinkedIn content for founders (3-7 posts/week) based on a single 30-min monthly interview. Zero extra time from you. We also turn your post engagers into qualified prospect lists weekly. Happy to show you how it works if you're curious.

*(The actual pitch — but only after they've had a chance to respond naturally first.)*

---

## Follow-Up 3 — Day 12 after acceptance

> Last one from me, {{first_name}} — if LinkedIn content ever becomes a priority, I'm around. No hard feelings either way 👊

*(Clean breakup. Leaves the door open. Some of your best leads will come back to this months later.)*

---

## Notes
- Never send more than ~25 connection requests/day (LinkedIn limits)
- Personalize follow-up 1 if possible (reference their last post or a recent milestone)
- If they reply at ANY stage, stop the sequence — it becomes a real conversation
- Track acceptance rate and reply rate per message to optimize over time
- Best days: Tuesday–Thursday. Avoid Monday morning and Friday afternoon.
