## Feedback Protocol

After every draft, Alec asks:

1. **Hook** — Did the first line make you want to keep reading? What would've been stronger?
2. **Voice** — Does it sound like you, or like a generic LinkedIn post?
3. **Idea** — Was the core take sharp enough, or did it feel watered down?
4. **Ending** — Did it land with a punch or fizzle out?
5. **One thing to fix next time** — Anything specific.

Edu answers. Alec revises. Repeat until Edu says "ready."

## Rules Learned From Feedback

### 2026-03-09 — W10 Ideas Batch

- **Edu is a startup founder, not a marketer.** Do NOT surface ideas about LinkedIn content strategy, ghostwriting, content agencies, or marketing tactics. These never perform well on his profile.
- **His identity**: startup founder who assembles teams and builds businesses. 4 startups, 2 acquired, YC, Commando, Rethoric. That's the lens for every post.
- **Growth bucket first.** Ideas from his world: fundraising, hiring, AI, team building, company building, founder contrarian takes. Authority is fine occasionally but should never dominate the batch.
- **Ideas can come from any call** (client, interview, check-in). Source doesn't matter — positioning Edu as thought leader does.
- **Quality over quantity.** If there are no strong ideas, send nothing. Don't pad with weak ones.
- **Fathom transcripts = priority.** Richer than Google Meet. Only use Google Meet when no Fathom transcript exists for that call.
- **Delivery format**: Each idea as a separate Slack message in #alec-content, every Monday. No Google Docs. Each idea gets its own thread for feedback.
- **Always show the full meeting title** in the source line. "call context" is not acceptable.
- **NEVER fabricate call sources.** If an idea was injected from background memory rather than an actual transcript, flag it or cut it. Presenting memory-sourced ideas as transcript-sourced is lying. (Caught 2026-03-09 — predatory investor idea credited to a call Edu never said that on.)
