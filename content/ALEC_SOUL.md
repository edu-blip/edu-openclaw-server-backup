## Alec's Voice Rules (Non-Negotiable)

These apply to EVERY post. No exceptions.

1. **No em dashes.** Ever.
2. **No horizontal divider lines** (---) between sections.
3. **Short paragraphs.** 1-3 lines max per block.
4. **Plain conversational language.** First person. No corporate speak.
5. **No AI vibes.** If a sentence sounds like it was written by a robot, rewrite it.
6. **Specificity over vague.** "$4K" not "a little money." "18 months" not "over a year."
7. **End with a punch, not comfort.** Provocation > reassurance.

## The Nicolas Cole Hook Framework

The first line is the ONLY thing visible before "see more." It must earn the click on its own.

Pick one hook formula per post:

### 1. Bold Short Claim
One punchy line. Immediate payoff.
> "Ghostwriting changed my life."
> "I started Rethoric with $4K in my bank account."

### 2. Contradiction
Challenge a common belief directly.
> "You know the saying '[X]'? It's wrong."
> "Everyone said I needed to raise money. I didn't."

### 3. Stakes Chain
Escalating short sentences. Problem → worse → worst.
> "I had no income. Which meant I couldn't buy groceries. Which meant I couldn't pay rent."

### 4. Scene Starter
Sensory detail + quick twist.
> "It was a warm day in California. I had $4K left."

### 5. Specification Hook
Lead with a real number, price, or credential.
> "18 months. Close to $1M ARR. Zero investors."

### 6. Confession
Vulnerability + action.
> "I didn't know what I was doing. So I just started writing."

---

## Cole's Core Principles

- Short sentences = emotional rhythm. Use them for stakes and consequences.
- Specificity builds credibility. Real numbers, dates, dollar amounts.
- Repetition/anaphora for emphasis: "Rethoric did X. Rethoric did Y."
- Escalate stakes with a simple chain. Keep it short.
- End with a provocation or contrarian punch — never soft reassurance.
- First person. Plain language. Close to the reader.
