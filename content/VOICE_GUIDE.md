## Edu's Vocabulary (Phrases That Sound Like Him)

Use these naturally when they fit. Don't force them.

- "Built and lost" (not just "failed")
- "Cobbler's shoes" / "cobbler's kids have no shoes"
- "Operator vs orchestrator"
- "Competing with a different ceiling"
- "I knew [X]. I still [did wrong thing]." — the confession pattern
- "The leverage exists." (declarative, not hedged)
- Numbers, always: "$40K MRR" not "decent revenue." "280 posts" not "a lot of posts."

## What Edu NEVER Says

These kill the voice immediately. Remove on sight.

- Em dashes (—)
- "Journey" / "authentic" / "vulnerable" / "powerful"
- "I'm grateful for..." as an opener or closer
- Soft endings ("Hope this helps!" / "Let me know what you think!")
- Bullet lists as the primary format (occasional is fine, not the default)
- Corporate jargon: "leverage synergies," "drive value," "ecosystem"
- Performative humility: "I'm no expert, but..."

## The Voice Filter (Run Every Post Through This)

Before finalizing any post, check:

1. **Uniqueness**: Could another founder have written this? If yes, add Edu's specific story.
2. **Specificity**: Are there real numbers, dates, dollar amounts? Vague = generic = forgettable.
3. **Tension**: Is there a contradiction or confession that makes him human?
4. **Contrarian edge**: Does it push back on something founders commonly believe?
5. **Ending**: Does it end with a punch or provocation? Not comfort. Not a question just to get comments.
6. **Voice**: No em dashes. No filler words. No AI vibes. Short paragraphs.

---

## Hook Formulas (Nicolas Cole Framework)

Pick one per post. The first line is the only thing visible before "see more."

1. **Bold Short Claim** — "The best founders aren't doing the work anymore."
2. **Contradiction** — "You know the saying '[X]'? It's wrong."
3. **Stakes Chain** — "[Problem]. Which meant [worse]. Which meant [worst]."
4. **Scene Starter** — Sensory detail + quick twist.
5. **Specification Hook** — Lead with a number, price, or credential.
6. **Confession** — "I didn't know [X]. So I did [Y]."
