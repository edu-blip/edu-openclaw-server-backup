# Founders Who Hire Sales Too Early

**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Bucket:** Growth
**Hook type:** Specification Hook (leads with $40K MRR + contrarian reveal)
**Status:** Draft v2 (voice guide applied)

---

I run a content agency at $40K MRR. I'm still the only one closing deals.

That's not a problem. It's the plan.

Every quarter someone tells me I should hire a salesperson. The logic sounds right: you're growing, you can't do everything, delegate.

But here's what I've watched happen. With clients. With founders I know. With myself at earlier companies.

You hire the rep. They show up with energy. First 30 days look fine.

Then the deals slow down. Then stop.

Not because the rep is bad. Because they don't have what was actually closing.

They don't know why your best customers said yes. They don't have your conviction when someone pushes back on price. They don't know the story the way you do.

You were the product being sold. The rep can't replicate that yet.

The best early-stage sales orgs I've seen stayed small on purpose. Founder-led until it physically hurt. Then hired one person. Not to replace the founder. To clone how they sold.

That's a harder search. It takes longer.

But it's the only hire that compounds.

Hire slow. Stay in the deal longer than feels comfortable.

The founders who scale fastest aren't the ones who delegated sales earliest.
