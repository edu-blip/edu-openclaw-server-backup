# AI Won't Replace Most Jobs

**Date:** 2026-03-11
**Bucket:** Growth
**Hook type:** Stakes Chain
**Source:** @emollick tweet (AI job impact limited by compute cost)
**Score:** 9/10

---

AI won't replace most jobs.

Not because it can't do the work.

Because it costs too much.

Running GPT-5 or Claude costs more per hour than a virtual assistant in Manila, a content writer in Lagos, or a data entry team in Bogotá.

"But open source models are free."

Sure. The model is free.

The Mac Studio you need to run it starts at $2,000. The version powerful enough for real coding work is $3,700. And even then you're running something that's still behind GPT-5 on complex tasks.

Want to use a Mac Mini instead? You get lightweight models that aren't going to replace anyone doing anything meaningful.

The economics only flip at the high end: coding, legal analysis, financial modeling. Work where the human alternative costs $150+/hr.

I've been building AI agents into my own business for months. The ROI is real.

But only in 2 or 3 specific places. Not everywhere.

Every founder telling you to "automate everything" either hasn't done the math or is running on VC money.

The winners will be the ones who identify the 3% of their workflow where AI genuinely beats humans on cost AND quality.

And ignore the rest.
