# The Outrage Was Never About the Environment

**Bucket:** Growth
**Hook type:** Bold Short Claim
**Status:** Ready to post

---

The outrage was never about the environment.

I see this pattern every few months. Different technology, same script.

2022: Bitcoin was destroying the planet. Before that: data centers. Before that: the internet itself.

Always the same move. Pick the newest thing. Attach an environmental cost. Save the world from your couch.

Here's a number nobody's building a movement around:

One golf course in Arizona uses 100 million gallons of water a year.

There are 16,000 golf courses in the US.

Zero viral threads. Zero movement.

Because golfers aren't threatening anything new.

Fresh water matters. Farming matters. And AI is already optimizing irrigation, predicting crop yields, cutting waste across supply chains.

The technology she wants people to stop supporting is actively solving the problems she says she cares about.

I've built 4 companies. The loudest opinions almost always come from people with the least context.

That tweet got 1.2 million views. Every one of them absorbed a take built on emotion and zero data.

Most of them believed it.

That's more dangerous than any data center.
