# Orchestrators, Not Operators (Final — Outreach Stack)

**Bucket:** Growth
**Hook type:** Confession
**Status:** Ready to post
**Source:** Team check-in (2026-03-02)

---

I hated doing outreach. So I stopped.

Not because I gave up. Because I built a system that does it without me.

RB2B flags which founders visited my website. Clay enriches and filters them by funding stage and company size. Apollo builds the list. Botdog sends the connection request on LinkedIn.

All of it runs in the background while I'm doing actual work.

I set it up once. Now it runs every day.

Most founders are doing this manually. Spending hours on Sales Navigator trying to find the right people. Writing connection requests one by one.

Those founders are operators. They trade time for pipeline.

The orchestrators built the system once and let it run.

Outreach was my #1 bottleneck for two years. I actively avoided it.

Now it's the one thing I never have to think about.

Build the machine. Then get out of its way.
